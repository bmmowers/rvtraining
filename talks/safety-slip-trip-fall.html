🛡 Safety

Slip, Trip & Fall Prevention

⏱ Est. 3 minutes

  1. Spills = immediate action. If you see a wet floor, a leaking hose, or product on the ground, stop and clean it up or block it off before anyone walks through. Don't assume someone else will handle it.
  2. Watch your path. Hoses, pallets, shrink wrap, and extension cords are the most common trip hazards on our floor. Walk around them or move them if you can do it safely.
  3. Footwear matters. Non-slip, closed-toe shoes are required for a reason. If your soles are worn smooth, it's time to replace them. Check the bottoms of your shoes today.
  4. Slow down at transitions. Moving between dry and wet areas (like from the warehouse into the cooler or wash area) is where most slips happen. Shorten your stride and watch the floor surface change.
  5. Report hazards you can't fix. Broken floor tiles, missing drain covers, and damaged mats should go to maintenance the same day. Use the Hub's Report an Issue button if needed.
