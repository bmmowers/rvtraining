🛡 Safety

Food Defense & Visitor Policy

⏱ Est. 3 minutes

  1. Every visitor must sign in and get a badge. No exceptions — drivers, vendors, repair techs, auditors, anyone who is not a River Valley employee. If someone arrives at your dock, they sign the visitor log at the driver entrance and receive a visitor badge before they go anywhere in the facility.
  2. Do not prop doors open. Access doors into the warehouse and food storage areas must stay closed and secured at all times. Propping a door open — even for "just a minute" — creates an uncontrolled access point and is a food defense violation. If you need airflow or frequent access, talk to your supervisor about the right solution.
  3. Challenge unfamiliar faces. If you see someone in the warehouse who you don't recognize and who isn't wearing a visitor badge, don't ignore it. Politely ask if you can help them, and notify your supervisor immediately. You are the first line of food defense on the floor.
  4. Drivers stay at the dock. Unless escorted by an employee, drivers should remain in the dock area. They should not be walking through storage aisles, break rooms, or office areas unaccompanied.
  5. Report anything that looks wrong. Tampered product, broken seals, unfamiliar packages, unlocked areas that should be secured — if something doesn't look right, say something. Report it to your supervisor or use the Report an Issue button in the Hub. It's always better to flag a false alarm than to miss a real threat.
Think about the last delivery we received. Did the driver sign in and get a badge? If not, what could we do differently to make sure that step doesn't get skipped?
